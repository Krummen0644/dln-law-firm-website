What is Medical Malpractice?

Medical malpractice occurs when a healthcare provider deviates from the accepted standard of care, causing injury or death to a patient. Not every bad outcome is malpractice, but when negligence is involved, victims deserve compensation.

To prove medical malpractice, we must show:

  • ✓ A doctor-patient relationship existed
  • ✓ The healthcare provider was negligent
  • ✓ The negligence caused your injury
  • ✓ You suffered measurable damages

Types of Medical Malpractice

Surgical Errors

Wrong-site surgery, retained instruments, anesthesia errors, nerve damage, unnecessary surgery

Misdiagnosis

Failure to diagnose cancer, heart attack, stroke, or other serious conditions leading to delayed treatment

Birth Injuries

Cerebral palsy, Erb's palsy, brain damage from oxygen deprivation, failure to perform C-section

Medication Errors

Wrong dosage, wrong medication, dangerous drug interactions, pharmacy mistakes

Hospital Negligence

Infections, bedsores, falls, inadequate staffing, failure to monitor vital signs

Delayed Treatment

Failure to recognize symptoms, ignoring test results, not ordering necessary tests
