Payment Terms & Disclaimer

Important information about payments and your legal relationship with our firm

Last Updated: November 10, 2025

No Attorney-Client Relationship

Making a payment through this portal does not create an attorney-client relationship between you and Donald L. Nageleisen Attorney at Law. An attorney-client relationship exists only when:

  • We have agreed in writing to represent you
  • A signed retainer agreement or engagement letter is in place
  • You have received written confirmation of representation from our office

This payment portal is for existing clients or those with approved payment arrangements only. If you're unsure whether you have an active attorney-client relationship with our firm, please contact us before making a payment.

Prior Results Disclaimer

Prior results do not guarantee or predict a similar outcome in any future case. Every legal matter is unique and depends on its own facts, circumstances, and applicable law. Past success in similar cases does not ensure similar results in your case.

Payment Processing

This website does not directly collect or process credit card information, bank account details, or other sensitive payment data. All payments are processed through secure third-party payment providers:

  • PayPal: Subject to PayPal's Terms of Service and Privacy Policy
  • Venmo: Subject to Venmo's Terms of Service and Privacy Policy
  • Square: Subject to Square's Terms of Service and Privacy Policy
  • Cash App: Subject to Cash App's Terms of Service and Privacy Policy

When you choose a payment method, you'll be directed to that provider's platform. Your payment information is entered directly on their secure servers and is protected by their security measures. We encourage you to review each provider's terms and privacy policies before making a payment.

Payment Application

Payments are typically applied to your account within 1-3 business days of receipt. To ensure your payment is applied correctly:

  • Always include your Case/Reference ID when making a payment
  • Include the memo or reference information provided during the payment process
  • Keep your payment confirmation receipt for your records

If your payment is not applied to the correct matter within 5 business days, please contact our billing department immediately.

Refunds are handled in accordance with Kentucky law and applicable Rules of Professional Conduct. If you believe you're entitled to a refund:

Fees and Costs

We do not charge additional fees for using the online payment portal. However, the payment provider you choose may charge their own transaction fees. Please review your chosen provider's fee schedule before completing your payment.

Payment Records

After making a payment, you will receive:

  • Immediate confirmation from the payment provider
  • An official receipt from our office via email within 1-2 business days
  • Updated account balance information (if applicable)

Keep all payment confirmations and receipts for your records. You may need them for tax purposes or future reference.

Confidentiality

Do not include confidential information, case details, medical information, or other sensitive data in the payment portal's notes field or in any payment memo. The payment portal is not a secure method for communicating privileged or confidential information.

If you need to discuss your case or share confidential information, please contact our office directly through secure channels.

Disputed Charges

If you have questions or concerns about charges on your account, please contact our billing department before making a payment. We're committed to transparent billing and will work with you to resolve any discrepancies.
