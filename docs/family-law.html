Family Law FAQs

How long does a divorce take in Kentucky?

Uncontested divorces can be finalized in 60-90 days. Contested divorces involving property division or custody disputes may take 6 months to over a year depending on complexity.

How is child custody determined?

Kentucky courts focus on the best interests of the child, considering factors like each parent's relationship with the child, stability, mental and physical health, and the child's preferences (if age-appropriate).

Can child support be modified?

Yes. If there's a material change in circumstances (job loss, income change, child's needs), you can petition the court to modify the support order.
